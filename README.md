# OpenNorth

**Private open-weight AI over your company data, with every answer traceable to source.**

In plain English: OpenNorth deploys AI on infrastructure the customer controls (their cloud account, a server in their building, or fully offline), grounded in their own documents, with every answer cited back to its source. Built in Sudbury, Ontario, aimed first at Northern Ontario's confidentiality-bound, document-heavy industries (mining supply & services first).

## Status

Solo-founder, pre-revenue, early stage. No customers, funding, or team yet, and the site says so plainly. The go-to-market opener is a **free proof of concept for the first Northern Ontario company** with a strong-fit use case.

## This repository

A static, single-file pitch website:

- [`index.html`](index.html): the entire site. No build step, no JavaScript, no external requests (fonts, icons, and diagrams are all inline). Open it directly in a browser, or serve it with anything (`python3 -m http.server`).
- [`PITCH-NOTES.md`](PITCH-NOTES.md): the working document behind the site: positioning rationale, cost estimate model with assumptions, an audit of which claims are strong vs. weak, and the real-world validation checklist.

## Site structure

Hero → Problem → When public AI is / isn't enough → What OpenNorth is → What "your own AI" means (and doesn't) → How it works (trust-boundary architecture) → Realistic today vs. not → First markets → Free Northern Ontario POC → Cost ranges & deployment options → Why now → Founder → FAQ → Sources.

All cost figures are planning ranges in CAD with stated assumptions, grounded in the public sources cited at the bottom of the page (as of July 2026); not quotes, and not invented precision.

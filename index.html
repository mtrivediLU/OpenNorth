The Problem

Every company has the data. Almost none can ask it a question.

The pain looks different at every company size — but the root cause is identical: data lives in disconnected systems, answers depend on who you ask, and the AI tools on the market either can't reach your data or can't be trusted with it.

Small · 5–50 people

"The numbers live in the owner's head."

  • Books in QuickBooks, sales in a spreadsheet, jobs in email — nothing talks to anything
  • No IT team, no data team, no one to even start an AI project
  • Per-seat AI subscriptions across staff outrun the software budget fast
  • ChatGPT answers from the internet — it knows nothing about your business
Medium · 50–500 people

"Monthly reporting takes a week of copy-paste."

  • Data scattered across ERP, CRM, accounting, and 50 spreadsheets
  • Someone rebuilds the same monthly report by hand, every month
  • Two departments answer the same question with two different numbers
  • Enterprise AI platforms are priced and built for firms 10× their size
Large · 500+ people

"We can't send that data to the cloud."

  • Operational data is sensitive, regulated, or safety-critical — cloud AI is off the table
  • Staff use ChatGPT anyway: ungoverned "shadow AI" with zero audit trail
  • Per-token API bills scale unpredictably — the more useful, the more expensive
  • When an AI answer drives a real decision, nobody can show where it came from

The Product

Ask. Trace. Report. One engine, three capabilities.

This is not "a chatbot." It is a governed answer layer over your own data — designed from day one so that every output can be audited, reproduced, and debugged.

Ask

Scoped, source-cited answers

Users pick the domain they're asking about — Finance, Sales, Operations, HR — and the AI answers only from the connected systems in that scope, with the source records cited on every answer. No internet guessing. No hallucinated numbers presented as fact.

Trace

Same question → same answer, company-wide

Recurring questions live in a versioned answer registry: one governed answer, regenerated from current data, with a logged trail of exactly which rows produced it and when it changed. If an answer looks wrong, you can debug it like code — not shrug at a black box. Role-based access controls decide who can see what.

Report

Reports that write themselves

Your monthly report becomes a live template. On schedule, the system pulls fresh data from every connected application, generates the report, and delivers it as PDF, email, or dashboard — humans review instead of assemble. Ad-hoc reports are one sentence away: "Give me Q2 sales by region as a PDF."

Our honesty policy: large language models are not deterministic by nature. "Same answer company-wide" is achieved by engineering, not magic — governed questions are versioned and served from the answer registry; ad-hoc questions always show their sources so they can be verified. We tell you exactly which is which. That discipline is the product.

FAQ

The questions everyone asks.

Is this just ChatGPT connected to my files?

No — three differences. Your model runs privately (your cloud or your hardware), so data never leaves your control. Every answer is grounded in your governed warehouse and cites its sources. And recurring questions are versioned in an answer registry, so the whole company gets one consistent, auditable answer — not whatever the model felt like saying today.

Do you actually train a model from scratch for us?

No, and nobody should sell you that. "Your company's AI model" means: an open-weight foundation model (MIT/Apache licensed) deployed privately for you, grounded in your data through retrieval, optionally fine-tuned on your vocabulary and documents. You get the ownership benefits without the eight-figure training bill.

Are open-source models actually good enough?

In 2026, yes — verifiably. GLM-5.2 (MIT license, June 2026) benchmarks within a point of the best closed models on demanding agentic tasks; Qwen, DeepSeek, and Llama fill out every size tier. For grounded Q&A and report generation over structured company data — the core of this product — right-sized open models are more than sufficient, and they improve every quarter at no cost to you.

What does it cost?

A one-time build (assessment, warehouse, deployment, automation) plus a flat annual support agreement. Hardware is either your cloud account or a GPU server you own. What you never pay: per-token fees, per-seat fees, or a bill that grows because your team used the tool more. We model your break-even against API pricing in the discovery call.

We already have a data warehouse. Do we start over?

No — that's the best starting position. We connect the AI layer directly to your existing warehouse (Snowflake, PostgreSQL, SQL Server, BigQuery…), add the retrieval, registry, RBAC, and reporting layers on top, and skip straight to deployment.

Can it really run with no internet?

Yes. The stack is proven in the most disconnected environment there is: underground mining. Our OreAcle proof-of-concept runs live sensor ingest, ML anomaly detection, and a local-LLM copilot fully offline. Your office is an easier problem.

Who sees what? We have confidential data.

Role-based access control is built into the retrieval layer itself — the model physically cannot retrieve data outside the asking user's scope. Every query is logged: who asked, what was retrieved, which model version answered. That log is your audit trail.
